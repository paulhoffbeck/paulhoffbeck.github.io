International Mobility

The engineering program includes 9 to 12 months of international mobility. It allows students to:

  • evolve in a culturally different environment;
  • develop an open-mindedness essential to the engineering profession;
  • build adaptability skills;
  • explore new working methods;
  • understand the global challenges faced by companies.

Here are some ideas and evidence of research on international mobility:

Target Countries

Taiwan

  • Global high-tech ecosystem (networks, cloud, semiconductors, IoT, 5G).
  • Numerous R&D centers and university-industry collaborations.
  • Ideal environment to develop technical and intercultural skills.

Austria

  • Dynamic in networks, cybersecurity, IoT.
  • Concrete projects through industrial partnerships.
  • Advanced digital infrastructure.

Switzerland

  • Leading technological ecosystem.
  • R&D centers, international companies, renowned universities (EPFL, ETH Zürich).
  • High technical standards and industrial excellence.

University Mobility

NCKU – National Cheng Kung University (Taiwan)

  • Excellence in engineering, computer science, and applied research.
  • Advanced laboratories + industrial partnerships.
  • Projects focused on semiconductors, networks, IoT, embedded systems.

NTHU – National Tsing Hua University (Taiwan)

  • Prestigious research university in Asia.
  • Specialized in AI, electronics, advanced networks.
  • Strong links with Hsinchu Science Park.

AAU – Alpen-Adria-Universität Klagenfurt (Austria)

  • Recognized for networks, cybersecurity, autonomous systems.
  • Modern laboratories and applied R&D projects.
  • Programs available in English.

Computing Program – FHNW (Switzerland)

  • Practical, industry-oriented approach.
  • Concrete projects and professional technologies.

Engineering Program – FHNW (Switzerland)

  • Electronics, embedded systems, automation, telecommunications.
  • Modern laboratories and real-world problem-solving.
