Professional Projects

These projects were carried out in a company as part of my second-year internship and my work-study program. They reflect my technical skills applied in a real professional context.

Second-Year Internship

Problem / Goals: The infrastructure of an industrial site was becoming obsolete, causing both security and performance issues. I was tasked with modernizing it.

Result/Indicator: 4 switches and over 200 ports to configure and connect

Role: I carried out the entire project independently

Technique: I started by listing the ports used, then studied the configurations of the old switches before reorganizing and reconfiguring the new ones. There was also a lot of work related to replacing the switches, as it is an industrial site.

Summary: Modernization of an industrial site's network: replacement of switches, rewiring, and updating technical documentation.

Configuration of an SD-WAN solution to improve network resilience and flexibility.

Work-Study Program

I will use the PORTE method to illustrate a short project I completed during my first period.

Problem / Goal: A client has a site with two distinct functions, which results in two separate fiber internet connections. For economic reasons, the client asked us to merge these two connections.

Result/Indicator: Nearly a hundred routers and 300 sites migrated

Role: I handled the configuration analysis and solution design: the plan, the protocol used, and the configuration file to be applied to the remote sites.

Technique: I studied the roles of the different sites and their configurations. I then explored what could be merged while keeping them as they were. Next, I designed the diagram and finally the configuration file. The deployment aspect is managed by a separate service.

This is a brief example of what I can achieve in my first year.

As a client solutions engineer, I participate in the design, deployment, and support of network, cloud, and security infrastructures. I will therefore be in contact with clients of different nationalities.
